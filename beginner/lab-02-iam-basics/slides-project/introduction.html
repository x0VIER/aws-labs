Understanding AWS Identity and Access Management

AWS Identity and Access Management (IAM) helps you securely control access to AWS resources.

IAM has four main components:

Users: Individual identities for people or services that need AWS access
Groups: Collections of users that share the same permissions
Policies: Documents that define permissions and specify what actions are allowed or denied
Roles: Sets of permissions that can be assumed by users or AWS services

IAM follows the principle of least privilege - granting only the permissions required to perform a task.
